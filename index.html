BMW X5

The BMW X5 is a mid-size luxury crossover produced by BMW. The first generation of the X5, with the chassis code E53, made its debut in 1999. It was BMW's first SUV and it also featured all-wheel drive and was available with either manual or automatic transmission. In 2006, the second generation X5 was launched, known internally as the E70, featuring the torque-split capable xDrive all-wheel drive system mated to an automatic transmission, and in 2009 the X5 M performance variant was released as a 2010 model.

Honda Civic eighth generation was introduced in Japan in September 2008. The creators called it not only a "revolutionary machine", which is radically different from its predecessors. In the Civil Environment, the concept of design, known as one moving form of motion ("One-stroke design"). Attractive smooth lines give it a streamlined look with a powerful and swift bottom of the body. The upper part of the body has a streamlined and dynamic shape, which passes into the windshield with the base shifted forward. In order for the salon to match the appearance of the car, a new concept was created - a "two-zone instrument panel". All instruments with a circular scale are located directly behind the steering wheel, within the peripheral vision.
